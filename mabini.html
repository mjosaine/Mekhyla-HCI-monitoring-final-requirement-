Apolinario Mabini

The Sublime Paralytic and Brains of the Revolution

Biography

Apolinario Mabini y Maranan (July 23, 1864 – May 13, 1903) was a Filipino revolutionary leader, lawyer, and statesman. Despite being paralyzed due to polio, Mabini played a pivotal role in the Philippine struggle for independence. Known as the "Brains of the Revolution" and "The Sublime Paralytic," he was a key adviser to President Emilio Aguinaldo and a strong advocate for true national sovereignty.

Born in Tanauan, Batangas, Mabini came from a humble background. His intelligence and diligence led him to pursue higher education despite financial difficulties. He later studied law at the University of Santo Tomas, where he was known for his intellect and dedication.

"True greatness consists in being great in little things." — Apolinario Mabini

Did You Know?

Mabini continued to write and serve the Filipino cause even after being struck by paralysis in 1896. He wrote much of his revolutionary work while bedridden.

Role in the Revolution

Mabini served as the first Prime Minister and Foreign Minister of the First Philippine Republic. He was a major force in crafting the political structure of the short-lived republic and was instrumental in drafting decrees and policies during its early days.

  • Advised Aguinaldo to establish a revolutionary government instead of a dictatorship
  • Drafted the "True Decalogue" — a guide for moral governance
  • Promoted civil liberties, education, and social reform
  • Opposed American colonization and fought for complete independence

Notable Works

La Revolución Filipina (1898)

Mabini's most important work, where he detailed the Philippine struggle for independence and outlined his political philosophy.

Other Contributions:

  • The True Decalogue: Ethical principles for Filipinos
  • Constitutional Plans: For a democratic republic
  • Letters and Essays: On patriotism, education, and governance

Timeline of Mabini's Life

  • July 23, 1864: Born in Tanauan, Batangas
  • 1881: Entered Colegio de San Juan de Letran
  • 1894: Admitted to the bar as a licensed lawyer
  • 1896: Paralyzed by polio
  • 1898: Became adviser to President Emilio Aguinaldo
  • 1899: Captured by American forces
  • 1901: Exiled to Guam for refusing to swear allegiance to the U.S.
  • May 13, 1903: Died of cholera shortly after returning to the Philippines

Legacy

Apolinario Mabini remains a symbol of intellect, integrity, and dedication to the Filipino nation:

  • Portrait on currency: Featured on the 10-peso bill (with Andrés Bonifacio)
  • Schools and universities: Named in his honor
  • Mabini Shrine: Preserved in Tanauan, Batangas
  • National Hero: Honored every July 23 (Mabini Day)
"He was the soul of the revolution." — General Emilio Aguinaldo on Mabini
