Marcelo H. del Pilar

The Great Propagandist

Biography

Marcelo Hilario del Pilar y Gatmaitán (August 30, 1850 – July 4, 1896) was a Filipino writer, lawyer, journalist, and revolutionary leader. A prominent figure in the *Propaganda Movement*, he used his powerful writings to campaign against Spanish abuses in the Philippines. He is best known as the second editor of the reformist newspaper La Solidaridad, which played a crucial role in awakening the Filipino nationalist spirit.

Born in Bulakan, Bulacan, del Pilar studied law at the University of Santo Tomas. Early in his life, he was already active in defending the rights of Filipinos, particularly against friar abuses. He later went into exile in Spain to escape persecution, continuing the fight through journalism and reformist campaigns.

"Where there is no equality, there is no liberty." — Marcelo H. del Pilar

Did You Know?

Marcelo del Pilar wrote biting satirical pieces under the pen name *Plaridel*, which became his iconic identity in Philippine literature and journalism.

Works and Contributions

  • La Solidaridad: Editor-in-chief from 1889 to 1895, spreading reformist ideals
  • Dasalan at Tocsohan: A satirical pamphlet criticizing friars' abuses
  • Diariong Tagalog: Founded the first bilingual newspaper in the country
  • Political Essays: Urged equal rights, representation, and secularization of parishes

Notable Writings

Dasalan at Tocsohan (1888)

A satirical piece mocking the friars, written in the style of prayer books to reveal their hypocrisy and abuses.

Other Works:

  • Caiigat Cayo: A response to Spanish apologists defending friars
  • La Frailocracia Filipina: Explores the oppressive power of friars
  • Pag-ibig sa Tinubuang Lupa: A nationalist poem encouraging Filipinos to love their homeland

Timeline of His Life

  • August 30, 1850: Born in Bulakan, Bulacan
  • 1878: Graduated with a law degree from UST
  • 1882: Founded *Diariong Tagalog*
  • 1888: Fled to Spain to escape persecution
  • 1889–1895: Edited La Solidaridad in Spain
  • 1896: Died of tuberculosis in Barcelona, Spain

Legacy

Marcelo H. del Pilar is remembered as one of the greatest minds of the Propaganda Movement and a fearless voice against colonial abuse:

  • Declared a National Hero by the Philippine government
  • Honored every August 30 (Del Pilar Day in Bulacan)
  • Marcelo H. del Pilar National High School and various streets named after him
  • His writings laid the foundation for Philippine nationalism
